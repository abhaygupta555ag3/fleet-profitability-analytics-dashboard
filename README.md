

# Fleet Profitability Analytics Dashboard

## Project Overview
End-to-end business analytics project built on real operational data from Cab Aggression — a self-drive car rental fleet business operated in Indore (2018–2021). This project analyses 42 months of fleet operations across 15 vehicles, covering revenue growth, cost management, channel strategy, and structured wind-down during COVID-19.

---

## Business Context
- **Business:** Cab Aggression — Self-drive and driver-attached car rental
- **Period:** March 2018 to August 2021 (42 months)
- **Fleet Size:** Scaled from 1 to 15 vehicles via reinvestment
- **Total Revenue:** Rs 3.5 Crore+ across 4 operational phases
- **Peak Monthly Revenue:** Rs 20L+ (March 2020)
- **Peak Monthly Profit:** Rs 3.5-4L (pre-COVID)
- **Wind-Down:** Zero bad debt | Zero unpaid driver dues | Rs 6-7L retained at closure

---

## Key Business Insights Analysed

### 1. Revenue and Profitability Trend
- Fleet grew 1 to 15 vehicles; monthly revenue scaled from Rs 45K to Rs 14L in Phase 1
- Profit margin peaked at 38% in Jan 2020 before COVID impact
- Phase 2 (Mar-May 2020): pivoted to tourist transport — highest ever revenue Rs 20L/month

### 2. Channel Strategy — Insurance Arbitrage Model
- Zoomcar partnership provided third-party insurance coverage worth Rs 25K-3.75L/month
- Gradually migrated from 100% Zoomcar to 50/50 split by Feb 2020
- Net benefit of Zoomcar channel tracked month-by-month vs direct bookings

### 3. Vehicle ROI Ranking
- Mahindra Scorpio and Hyundai Creta delivered highest net contribution (Rs 55-58K/month)
- Ford Ecosport and Hyundai Verna identified as low-ROI — sold first during wind-down
- Decision to sell worst performers first preserved cash flow for 4+ additional months

### 4. Wind-Down Analysis
- 15 vehicles disposed across 3 phases (Oct 2020, Mar 2021, Aug 2021)
- Overall recovery rate: 25-54% of purchase price depending on vehicle and timing
- Strategic sequencing of sales minimised total losses

## Key Findings

1. Selling low-ROI vehicles first (Verna, Ecosport) during COVID extended business survival by 4+ months and preserved Rs 62K/month in EMI savings.

2. Zoomcar insurance arbitrage saved Rs 25K-3.75L/month in third-party insurance costs — making the commission worth paying in early fleet-building phase.

3. Fleet utilisation stayed near 100% in Indore student market by targeting semester-end travel demand — seasonal booking pattern identified and exploited.

4. Managed wind-down recovered Rs 29L across all vehicle disposals with zero bad debt and zero unpaid driver dues despite COVID pressure.
